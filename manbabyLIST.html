Man Vs Baby

S1 E4

Genre: Comedy

After a disastrous experience house sitting a high-tech mansion hampered by an inconvenient insect, Trevor Bingley is now looking after a luxurious London penthouse, with another unlikely, uncalled-for companion.

Show Reviews-

finn ⭐️7/10



I think some people forget that not every show is meant to be tailored to their tastes. Man vs Baby is a family comedy, not an edgy adult one, and that's the point. It's made to be light, safe, and funny for all ages, especially kids. The humour is lighthearted, occasionally cringey, and yeah, it can be frustrating watching the main character make one silly mistake after another, but that's by design. It leans into nostalgia and it's meant to make you both roll your eyes and smile. It's not meant to be a masterpiece or a show to remember forever. Don't take it too seriously. If you go in knowing it's a goofy, feel-good Christmas special for the whole family, you'll probably enjoy it a lot more!

SRIM ⭐️7/10



'Man vs Baby' is predictable and a bit absurd and you absolutely have to suspend disbelief, yet I still had a good time because the real selling point is Rowan Atkinson doing what only he can do. There's this specific old-school Bean energy in the way he reacts to chaos with those tiny pauses, the confused stares, the sudden stubborn determination and it honestly felt nostalgic to watch him carry scenes with pure physical comedy instead of loud punchlines.

andrea ⭐️1/10
